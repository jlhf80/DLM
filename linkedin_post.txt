Dynamic Linear Models are one of the more underused tools in applied forecasting.
They handle trend, seasonality, time-varying coefficients, missing data, and
structural breaks — all in a unified probabilistic framework with uncertainty
built in.

I put together a three-tier notebook series covering the full methodology:

▸ Beginner — Kalman filter, RTS smoother, LLT, seasonal models, MLE, PyMC intro
▸ Intermediate — Discount factors, conjugate V estimation, dynamic regression, Fourier seasonal, model comparison
▸ Advanced — FFBS / Gibbs sampling, intervention analysis, structural break monitoring, multivariate + missing data

All implemented from scratch in pure NumPy. Grounded in West & Harrison's
Bayesian Forecasting and Dynamic Models and Petris et al.'s Dynamic Linear
Models with R.

Worked solutions included for all 44 exercises across the three tiers.

Repo in the comments (notebooks pre-executed — no setup needed to read through).
